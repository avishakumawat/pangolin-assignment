Diagnosis
Pick one core problem. Support it. Make it sharp.
Core Diagnosis — One Statement
This is not a traffic problem. Not a CVR problem.
It is an ICP targeting problem — every channel, every keyword, every ad is optimised to attract people who will never have the authority or urgency to buy.

Every signal in this engagement traces back to one upstream failure. The previous strategy was built around content volume and audience breadth — treating "HR" as a category rather than isolating the specific buyer with budget authority. The machine is working perfectly. For the wrong people.

Task 3
Content Ownership Model
What breaks when junior strategists own content — and how to build a system that prevents it at scale.
The Belief That Breaks Content at Scale

Junior strategists who evaluate content on craft criteria — "Is this well-written? Does it flow?" — will consistently produce content that is readable and strategically inert. The failure mode is not bad writing. It is strategic disconnection: content that educates instead of converting, speaks to everyone instead of the ICP, and cannot be tied to any pipeline outcome. At scale, this creates an operation that looks productive and generates no commercial signal.

The specific gap is the absence of a conversion-oriented success standard. The standard required is not "is this good writing?" but: did this content move the right person toward a decision? That is the only question that matters. The system's job is to make that question impossible to ignore at every stage of production.

🎯
The Brief as a Positioning Document
Every content asset requires a mandatory brief answering four questions before a word is written: (1) Who exactly is this for — specific ICP tier? (2) What decision does this support? (3) What single action should the reader take? (4) What does success look like in measurable terms? A brief that cannot answer all four is returned. Strategic intent gates production — it is never the afterthought.
✓
Two-Stage QA — Strategic, Then Editorial
Stage 1 (self-review): Does the opening address a specific ICP pain? Does every section serve the brief's stated decision? One clear next action? Tone calibrated to seniority? Stage 2 (lead review — strategic only): Reviewer reads with the brief open. Not line-editing prose — checking strategic alignment. One round of substantive notes. The strategist owns the prose; the system owns the strategy.
📊
Content Tied to Funnel Metrics
Every content asset is tagged to a funnel stage and a specific KPI on publication: awareness-stage → qualified ICP visitor rate; lead magnets → CVR + lead seniority; nurture emails → click-to-demo conversion. Strategists review these numbers in weekly 1-on-1s and must explain variance — not just report it. Explaining five underperforming pieces develops more reliable instincts than completing ten writing courses.
🔁
30-Day Retrospective Loop
Every significant content asset gets a formal 30-day retrospective: what was the hypothesis, what happened in the data, what would be briefed differently now. Documented in the knowledge base. Becomes a training reference for future strategists. Over 12 months, this creates a body of evidence across client verticals that new hires can onboard from in their first two weeks — rather than repeating known, costly mistakes.
Governing Principle

Content quality at scale is not a function of individual writing ability. It is a function of how precisely the system defines what "good" means before production begins, and how rigorously it measures outcomes after. A system that makes strategically aligned content easier to produce than strategically misaligned content — regardless of who is writing — is the only version that holds at scale.

Signature Growth Idea
The HRIS Job Posting Trigger System

When a mid-market company posts a job listing for "HR Analytics Manager," "People Analytics Lead," or "HRIS Specialist," they are advertising a buying intent signal louder than any keyword search. They have a defined problem, a budget allocation in progress, and a decision-maker actively thinking about analytics infrastructure.

The idea: build an automated pipeline that monitors job boards (LinkedIn, Indeed, Greenhouse) for these specific job titles at companies matching the ICP, then triggers a personalised ABM sequence within 24 hours of the posting going live — before any competitor has seen the signal.

How It Works — 4-Step System
1
Signal detection
Zapier or Clay monitors LinkedIn Jobs + Indeed for titles: "HR Analytics," "People Analytics," "HRIS Manager" at companies with 200–1,000 employees. Filters by industry. Fires when a new posting matches.
2
Account enrichment
Apollo or Clay enriches the company — finds the VP HR or CHRO, verifies LinkedIn profile is active, checks if they're already in CRM. If net new: adds to ABM sequence queue.
3
Trigger-personalised outreach
Message references the specific job posting (as shown in the ABM example above). Sent within 24 hours of posting going live. Reply rates on trigger-based messages are 3–5× higher than cold sequences — because the relevance is undeniable.
4
Retarget with LinkedIn ads
Simultaneously, the VP's company is added to a LinkedIn Matched Audience. The Benchmark Report ad starts serving to them as a parallel touchpoint — warm outbound + paid retargeting hitting the same person in the same week.
